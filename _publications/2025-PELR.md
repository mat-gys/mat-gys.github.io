excerpt: "Is it better to be a big fish in a small pond or a small fish in a big pond? We study how initial rank affects short- and long-run effort using a natural experiment in a high-effort setting. Using an RDD around cutoffs, we find that individuals assigned to a lower quality group exert a higher effort at the start but, by the end of the task, show no differences in performance with respect to those assigned to a higher quality group, which suggests burnout. We are actively developing a dynamic model of effort choice and Bayesian learning."
