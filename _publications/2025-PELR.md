excerpt: "We study peer effects in marathon running using a regression discontinuity design around wave assignment cutoffs. Runners quasi-randomly assigned to a lower (faster) wave run faster at the start but burn out later in the race, leading to similar finish times relative to those assigned to the higher wave — but with greater variability in effort. This suggests that peer-induced pacing has real performance consequences, even when average outcomes appear unchanged."
